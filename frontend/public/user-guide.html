1 · Getting started

Signing in

  1. Open https://inspecta.isiri.rw in your web browser (Chrome, Edge, Safari or Firefox).
  2. Click Sign In and enter the email and password your administrator gave you.
  3. You’ll land on the Dashboard, and the guided tour will start automatically the first time.
📱 Works anywhere Inspecta BUILDOS runs in any modern browser on desktop, tablet or phone. You can also “install” it as an app from your browser menu for quick access.

What you can see depends on your role

Each person is given a role. The menu only shows the areas your role is allowed to use.

RoleTypically can do
System AdministratorEverything, plus manage users, roles and company settings.
Project ManagerMost modules: planning, finance, production, procurement, HR, payroll, POS, approvals.
Site EngineerProduction, QA/QC, HSE, field ops, scheduling, inventory (read).
Quantity SurveyorPlanning, finance, procurement, profitability, payroll (read).
StorekeeperInventory, procurement and Point of Sale.

2 · Understanding the screen

  • Left sidebar — the main menu. Click any item to open that area (module).
  • Top bar — global search, Ask AI (the Copilot), the “?” Help button (replays the tour), theme switch, the 🔔 notifications bell, and your profile / sign-out.
  • Project selector — inside most modules there’s a dropdown at the top right to choose which project you’re working on. Pick a project before adding records.
  • Summary cards — the coloured number tiles at the top of a module show live totals (e.g. Total Sales, Active Employees).
⚠️ “Select a project to continue” Many screens are project-specific. If a table says this, choose a project from the dropdown at the top first.

4 · Projects, Clients & Contracts

Menu → Planning Suite

Add a client

  1. Open Planning Suite → Clients tab.
  2. Click + Add Client.
  3. Enter the name, choose Client Type (private / government / individual — this affects billing), add contact details and TIN, then Create.

Create a project

  1. Open Planning Suite → Project Setup tab → + Add Project.
  2. Fill in the code, name, type, the client, the contract value (in RWF), start & planned finish dates, and location.
  3. Click Create. The project now appears in the project selector everywhere.

Record the contract

On the Contracts tab, add the contract reference, type (lump sum / unit price / cost-plus), retention %, and signed date.

5 · Planning — WBS & BOQ

Menu → Planning Suite

The Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) is the list of activities; the Bill of Quantities (BOQ) prices them.

  1. Select your project at the top.
  2. On the WBS tab, add items (e.g. “1.0 Substructure”, “2.1 Concrete Frame”), each with a unit and planned quantity.
  3. On the BOQ tab, add priced items — quantity × rate gives the amount automatically.
💡 Bulk load from Excel Use the Excel Import / Export panel at the top of Planning to export a template, fill it in, and import many WBS/BOQ rows at once.

6 · Human Resources

Menu → Human Resources

Add an employee

  1. Open Human Resources → Employees → + Add Employee.
  2. Enter full name, national ID, trade, the crew and project they belong to, and contact details.
  3. For payroll, set Gross Monthly Salary (RWF) and Medical Scheme (RAMA / private / none). Click Create.

Use the other tabs to manage Trades, Wage Rates, Crews (with a foreman), and Crew Members.

7 · Recording Attendance

Menu → Field Ops → Attendance

Attendance records who worked, on which day — and it feeds payroll.

  1. Open Field Ops from the menu and choose the Attendance tab.
  2. Select the project at the top right.
  3. Click + Add Attendance.
  4. Pick the employee (or type a worker name), set the date, choose the status — present / half day / absent / leave — and enter hours worked.
  5. Click Create. The record appears in the list and the Total Hours card updates.
🔁 Daily routine Record attendance each day. To review a period, use the From / To date filter, filter by status, and click Excel to export the timesheet.
💡 Why it matters When you run payroll, employees who have qualifying attendance in the month are the ones paid — so keeping attendance current keeps payroll accurate.

8 · Payroll (Rwanda PAYE / RSSB)

Menu → Payroll

Payroll calculates each employee’s tax and social-security deductions and produces payslips.

One-time setup: statutory rates

  1. Open Payroll → Statutory Rates tab.
  2. Confirm the PAYE bands and RSSB rates (pension, maternity, medical, CBHI). These come pre-loaded but your administrator can update them — they are date-versioned, so old payslips stay correct.

Run a monthly payroll

  1. Make sure the month’s attendance and employee salaries are up to date.
  2. Open Payroll → Payroll Runs → set the period month → Create payroll run.
  3. On the new run, click Compute. The system calculates gross, PAYE, RSSB and net pay for every active employee and generates payslips.
  4. Click the run to expand it and review each payslip (gross, PAYE, deductions, net).
  5. When satisfied, click Post. This locks the run and records the net pay as a cash outflow in Finance.
⚠️ Posting is final A posted run can’t be recomputed. Review payslips before posting.

9 · Point of Sale (POS)

Menu → Point of Sale

Sell materials, equipment rental or services over the counter, with VAT receipts and cash reconciliation.

Set up products (once)

On the Products tab, add what you sell — name, type, price (RWF), and whether VAT applies. Link a product to a stock material so sales draw it down automatically.

Make a sale

  1. Open Point of Sale → Till & Sales.
  2. Click Open till and enter the opening cash float.
  3. Tap products to add them to the cart; adjust quantities with + / −.
  4. Enter the client name (or leave “Walk-in”), choose the payment method (cash / mobile money / bank), and click Complete Sale. A receipt with VAT is created.

Close the till

  1. At end of day, enter the counted cash and click Close till.
  2. The system compares it to expected cash and flags any variance.

Use the Service Invoices tab for billed services (e.g. testing) outside the till.

10 · Equipment, Fuel & Usage

Menu → Equipment
  • Register — add machines (code, category, ownership, daily rate, fuel type, home project).
  • Fuel Logs — record litres and cost per litre; total cost is calculated. Use the date filter to see consumption over a period.
  • Usage Logs — record daily hours used, by operator, allocated to a project/activity.
  • Maintenance — schedule and log services, costs and downtime.
  • Utilization — planned vs available hours.

11 · Inventory & Stock

Menu → Inventory
  1. Materials — add each material (code, unit, reorder level, unit cost).
  2. Stock Movements — record opening balances, receipts, issues and adjustments. The running balance is kept automatically.
  3. Goods Received (GRN) — log deliveries against a purchase order; this posts a receipt to the ledger.
  4. Material Issues — issue stock to a project/activity; this posts an issue and allocates the cost.
🔔 Low-stock alerts When a material drops to its reorder level, the system raises a notification automatically.

12 · Procurement

Menu → Procurement

The buying chain: Purchase Request → Purchase Order → Goods Received.

  1. Suppliers — add your vendors (name, category, TIN, payment terms).
  2. Purchase Requests — raise a request, then use Submit → it is Approved/Rejected by an approver, then Ordered.
  3. Purchase Orders — create the order (optionally from a request) for a supplier.
  4. Deliveries / GRNs — record what arrives.
  5. RFQs — request quotes from several suppliers and award the best.

13 · Production & Daily Reports

Menu → Production
  1. Open Production and pick the project.
  2. On Daily Reports, create the day’s report (date, weather), then add activity lines — planned vs actual quantity, labour hours and equipment hours per activity.
  3. The analytics at the top show productivity, variance and Earned Value (CPI/SPI). Big shortfalls raise an alert automatically.

There is also a quick Daily Production Entry screen for fast logging from site, including voice input.

14 · Scheduling (Gantt / CPM)

Menu → Scheduling (CPM)
  1. Add activities with durations and start dates.
  2. Add dependencies (which activity follows which).
  3. The system computes the critical path and shows a Gantt chart, highlighting delayed and critical tasks.

15 · Finance & IPC Billing

Menu → Finance
  • Budget — set budgeted amounts by activity and cost category (labour / material / equipment / subcontract).
  • Actual Costs — record costs as they’re incurred. The summary shows budget vs actual and flags overruns.
  • Invoices / IPC — raise client invoices or Interim Payment Certificates (gross valuation, retention, advance, tax → net is calculated). Track status from draft → certified → paid.
  • Payments — record money received against invoices.
  • Cash Flow — inflows vs outflows, with a monthly curve and deficit warnings.

16 · Quality (QA / QC)

Menu → QA/QC
  1. Inspections — log inspections with a pass/fail result.
  2. NCR Register — when something fails, raise a Non-Conformance Report (description, severity, rework cost) linked to the inspection.
  3. Material Tests and Rework — record lab tests and any rework with its cost.
🔔 Auto-alerts New or critical NCRs and failed tests notify the team automatically.

17 · Health & Safety (HSE)

Menu → HSE
  • Incidents — log near-misses, first aid, lost-time injuries (type, severity, involved person).
  • Risk Assessments — activity-based assessments with validity dates.
  • Toolbox Talks — record safety talks and attendance.
  • PPE Checks — pass/fail checks per worker.

The safety dashboard shows incident frequency rate and a safety score.

18 · Risk Register

Menu → Risk Register

Log project risks with a probability (1–5) and impact (1–5); the score (probability × impact) is colour-coded. Record the mitigation and owner, and track status.

19 · Approvals

Menu → Approvals

Requests that need sign-off (e.g. purchase requests) appear here. Approvers click Approve or Reject with a note; the requester is notified.

20 · Documents

Menu → Documents

A versioned register for drawings, certificates and contracts. Add a document with its category and file link; new versions keep the history.

21 · Reports & Exports

Menu → Reports · or the Excel/CSV button on any list
  • From the Reports page, download portfolio and per-project reports as Excel, CSV or PDF.
  • From any list, click Excel or CSV to export exactly what you’re viewing (your search, filters and dates are applied).

22 · AI Copilot

Menu → AI Copilot · or “Ask AI” in the top bar
  1. Open the Copilot and type a question in plain language, e.g. “Which project has the worst cost variance?” or “Summarise this month’s payroll.”
  2. The Copilot answers using only your live data — it never invents figures, and shows a confidence level.
💡 Try asking “Show under-performing activities”, “Any low-stock materials?”, “Open NCRs and their cost”.

23 · Dashboards & Portfolio

  • Dashboard — your home page: project health, KPIs and AI insights.
  • Executive Intelligence — AI-blended company-wide metrics for leadership.
  • Portfolio — compare all projects side by side (progress, cost variance, top risk).
  • Planning Dashboards — resource, labour and material planning roll-ups.

24 · Administration Admins only

Menu → Administration
  • Users — invite team members and set their role.
  • Roles — view exactly what each role can access.
  • Company Settings — name, currency (RWF), TIN, logo, working days.
  • Audit Trail — a record of who created, changed or deleted what.

25 · Tips & FAQ

I don’t see a menu item

Your role may not have access to it. Ask your administrator to adjust your role.

A list says “select a project”

Choose a project from the dropdown at the top right of the screen.

How do I see the tour again?

Click the “?” (Help) button in the top bar at any time.

Can I export my data?

Yes — every list has Excel and CSV buttons, and the Reports page produces PDFs.

What currency is used?

Rwandan Francs (RWF) throughout.

Is my data safe?

Access is controlled by role, every change is recorded in the audit trail, and your company’s data is kept separate from others.
