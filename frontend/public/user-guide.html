Welcome! This guide walks you through everything Inspecta can do — from creating your company account and setting up a project to recording production, running payroll, selling at the till, and reading your dashboards. No technical knowledge needed; just follow the numbered steps.

💡 The fastest way to learn The app has a built-in guided tour. After you sign in, it can walk you through every area. You can start it anytime by clicking the “?” (Help) button in the top bar.

1 · Getting started & sign-up

Create your company account (first time)

Inspecta is self-service: the first person from a company signs up, which creates the company (organisation) and makes them its System Administrator. Everyone else is invited afterwards from inside the app.

  1. Open https://inspecta.isiri.rw in your web browser (Chrome, Edge, Safari or Firefox).
  2. Click Sign up and enter your Company name, your Full name, Email and a password (at least 8 characters). Click Create account.
  3. We send a verification email. Open it and click “Verify my email.” The link is valid for 24 hours. (Didn’t get it? Use Resend on the sign-in screen, and check spam.)
  4. Verifying activates your account and signs you straight in — you land on the Dashboard. No second login needed the first time.
⚠️ You must verify before you can sign in Until the email is verified, the account is dormant and sign-in is refused with “Please verify your email.” Verify first, then you can log in normally afterwards.

Signing in (returning users)

  1. Go to the app and click Sign in.
  2. Enter your email and password. You’ll return to the Dashboard.
📱 Works anywhere Inspecta runs in any modern browser on desktop, tablet or phone. You can also “install” it as an app from your browser menu for quick access, and it keeps showing cached data if your connection drops.

Inviting your team (admins)

Once you’re in, open Administration → Users to invite colleagues and give each one a role. Their role decides which menu items and actions they see.

2 · Understanding the screen

  • Left sidebar — the main menu, organised into collapsible groups: Planning & Scheduling Operations People Finance Supply Chain Quality & Safety Workflow & Records. Click a group to expand it, then click an item to open it.
  • Top bar — global search, Ask AI (the Copilot), the “?” Help button (starts the tour), the 🔔 notifications bell, and your profile / sign-out.
  • Project selector — inside project-based modules there’s a dropdown at the top to choose which project you’re working on. Pick a project before adding records.
  • Summary cards — the coloured number tiles at the top of a module show live totals (e.g. Budget, Active Employees, Total Sales).
⚠️ “Select a project to continue” Many screens are project-specific. If a table says this, choose a project from the dropdown at the top first.

4 · Projects, Clients & Contracts

Menu → Planning & Scheduling → Planning Suite

Add a client

  1. Open Planning Suite → Clients tab.
  2. Click + Add Client.
  3. Enter the name, choose Client Type (private / government / individual), add contact person, phone, email, TIN (tax number) and address, then Create.

Create a project

  1. Open Planning Suite → Project Setup tab → + Add Project.
  2. Fill in the name, project type & category, the client, the project manager, the contract value (RWF) and planned profit margin, start & planned finish dates, location and site surfaces. The project code is generated automatically.
  3. Click Create. The project now appears in the project selector everywhere.

Record the contract

On the Contracts tab, add the contract reference and number, link it to the client & project, choose the type (lump sum / unit price / cost-plus / time & material), and set retention %, advance payment, defects-liability months and signed date.

5 · Planning — WBS & BOQ

Menu → Planning & Scheduling → Planning Suite

The Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) is the list of activities; the Bill of Quantities (BOQ) prices them.

  1. Select your project at the top.
  2. On the WBS tab, add items (e.g. “1.0 Substructure”, “2.1 Concrete Frame”), each with a unit, planned quantity, level and weight %. Set a Parent to build the hierarchy, or use the WBS Tree tab to see it nested.
  3. On the BOQ tab, add priced items — quantity × rate gives the amount automatically; add markup and contingency %. Use BOQ Versions to keep revisions.
  4. On the Productivity tab, set planned productivity rates (units per crew-day) so production can be measured against a benchmark.
💡 Bulk load from Excel Use the Excel Import / Export panel at the top of Planning to export a template, fill it in, and import many WBS/BOQ rows at once.

6 · Human Resources

Menu → People → Human Resources

Add an employee

  1. Open Human Resources → Employees → + Add Employee.
  2. Enter full name, national ID, trade, the crew and project they belong to, and contact details. The employee number is generated automatically.
  3. For payroll, set the Gross Monthly Salary (RWF) and Medical Scheme (RAMA / private / none), and set Status to active. Click Create.

Use the other tabs to manage Trades, Wage Rates (daily/hourly by trade), Crews (with a foreman), Crew Members and daily Availability. The Website Team tab controls who appears on your public marketing site.

7 · Field Ops & Attendance

Menu → Operations → Field Ops

Field Ops is the site-level record: a daily Site Diary, Tasks, and Attendance.

Record attendance

  1. Open Field Ops and choose the Attendance tab.
  2. Select the project at the top.
  3. Click + Add Attendance.
  4. Enter the worker name, their trade, the hours worked and the date, then Create. The record appears in the list and the Total Hours card updates.
🔁 Daily routine Record attendance each day. To review a period, use the From / To date filter, filter by status (present / half day / absent / leave), and click Excel to export the timesheet.

Site Diary & Tasks

  • Site Diary — one entry per day with weather, workforce count and notes.
  • Tasks — assign work with a status (To do / In progress / Done / Blocked) and due date.

8 · Payroll (Rwanda PAYE / RSSB)

Menu → People → Payroll

Payroll calculates each employee’s tax and social-security deductions and produces payslips.

One-time setup: statutory rates

  1. Open Payroll → Statutory Rates tab.
  2. Confirm the PAYE bands and RSSB rates (pension, maternity, medical, CBHI). They are date-versioned, so updating them keeps old payslips correct.

Run a monthly payroll

  1. Open Payroll → Payroll Runs, choose the period month, and click Create payroll run.
  2. On the new run click Compute. Every active employee who has a Gross Monthly Salary is included; the system works out gross, PAYE, RSSB and net pay and generates payslips.
  3. Expand the run to review each payslip (gross, PAYE, deductions, net).
  4. When satisfied, click Post. This locks the run (status POSTED) and records the net pay as a cash outflow in Finance.
ℹ️ Attendance pro-rating If attendance is recorded against employees for that month, pay is pro-rated over a 26-working-day month; otherwise the full monthly salary is paid.
⚠️ Posting is final A posted run can’t be recomputed. Review payslips before posting.

9 · Point of Sale (POS)

Menu → Finance → Point of Sale

Sell materials, equipment rental or services over the counter, with VAT receipts and cash reconciliation.

Set up products (once)

On the Products tab, add what you sell — name, type (material / equipment rental / service), unit price (RWF), and whether VAT applies. Link a product to a stock material so sales draw it down automatically.

Make a sale

  1. Open Point of Sale → Till & Sales.
  2. Click Open till and enter the opening cash float.
  3. Tap products to add them to the cart; adjust quantities with + / −.
  4. Enter the client name (or leave “Walk-in”), choose the payment method, and click Complete Sale. A receipt with VAT is created and any linked stock is reduced.

Close the till

  1. At end of day, enter the counted cash and click Close till.
  2. The system compares it to expected cash and flags any variance.

Use the Service Invoices tab for billed services (e.g. testing) outside the till, with their own status (Pending / Paid / Overdue / Cancelled).

10 · Equipment, Fuel & Usage

Menu → Operations → Equipment
  • Register — add machines (name, category, ownership, status, daily/hourly rate, fuel type, home project). The code is auto-generated.
  • Categories — group your equipment.
  • Fuel Logs — record litres and cost per litre; total cost is calculated. Use the date filter to see consumption over a period.
  • Usage Logs — record daily hours used, by operator, allocated to a project/activity.
  • Maintenance — schedule and log services, costs and downtime (Scheduled / Done / Overdue).
  • Utilization — planned vs available hours, with a utilization %.

11 · Inventory & Stock

Menu → Supply Chain → Inventory
  1. Materials — add each material (code, unit, category, reorder level, unit cost, supplier).
  2. Stock Movements — record opening balances, receipts, issues, adjustments, transfers, returns and waste. The running balance is kept automatically.
  3. Material Planning — plan required quantities per project with lead times.
  4. Goods Received (GRN) — log deliveries (optionally against a purchase order); confirm once you attach the signed delivery note.
  5. Material Issues — issue stock to a project/activity; confirm against the signed issue slip. This allocates the cost.
🔔 Low-stock alerts The Inventory summary shows stock value, reorder alerts, dead stock and material waste %, and valuation by Weighted Average or FIFO. When a material drops to its reorder level, a notification is raised automatically.

12 · Procurement

Menu → Supply Chain → Procurement

The buying chain runs a Purchase Request through a clear approval workflow, then into orders and deliveries.

  1. Suppliers — add your vendors (name, category, TIN, payment terms, rating, lead time).
  2. Purchase Requests — raise a request, then use the buttons on the workflow panel: Submit → Approve/Reject → Order → Mark Delivered → Close.
  3. Purchase Orders — create the order for a supplier (optionally from an approved request).
  4. RFQs & Quotes — request quotes from several suppliers and award the best one.
  5. Deliveries — record what arrives against a purchase order.
💡 MRP The Procurement summary includes Material Requirements Planning — pick a project to see net requirements (planned − stock on hand) and the estimated cost to procure.

13 · Production & Daily Reports

Menu → Operations → Production
  1. Open Production and pick the project.
  2. On Daily Reports, create the day’s report (report number, date, shift, weather). Then on Daily Entries add activity lines — WBS activity, planned vs actual quantity, labour hours and equipment hours.
  3. Use Material Use to record planned vs used quantities and waste per entry.
  4. The analytics at the top show productivity, variance and Earned Value (CPI/SPI). Big shortfalls raise an alert automatically.

There is also a quick full-screen Daily Production Entry screen for fast logging from site.

14 · Scheduling (Gantt / CPM)

Menu → Planning & Scheduling → Scheduling (CPM)
  1. Pick the project. On Activities, add tasks with a code, duration and start date (mark milestones where relevant). Link each to a WBS item.
  2. On Dependencies, connect activities — choose the relationship (FS / SS / FF / SF) and any lag in days.
  3. The system computes the critical path and shows a Gantt chart, highlighting critical tasks, plus a table of early/late start & finish and float.

15 · Finance & IPC Billing

Menu → Finance → Finance
  • Budget — set budgeted amounts by activity and cost category (labour / material / equipment / subcontractor / overhead), with types Original / Revised / Forecast / Contingency.
  • Actual Costs — record costs as they’re incurred. The summary shows budget vs actual, cost variance and forecast profit, and flags overruns.
  • Cash Flow — log inflows and outflows (actual or forecast) by category.
  • Invoices / IPC — raise client invoices or Interim Payment Certificates (gross valuation, previously certified, retention, advance deduction, tax → net is calculated). Track status from Draft → Submitted → Certified → Approved → Paid.
  • Payments — record money received against invoices (reference auto-generated).

16 · Quality (QA / QC)

Menu → Quality & Safety → QA/QC
  1. Inspections — log inspections with a Pass / Fail / Pending result and defect count.
  2. Material Tests — record lab tests (concrete, soil, asphalt, steel…) with batch, result and certificate.
  3. NCR Register — when something fails, raise a Non-Conformance Report (description, severity, root cause, corrective action) linked to the WBS activity.
  4. Corrective Actions and Rework — track action plans and any rework with its cost and delay impact.
  5. Compliance Docs — method statements, ITPs, permits and certifications with expiry dates.
🔔 Auto-alerts New or critical NCRs and failed tests notify the team automatically.

17 · Health & Safety (HSE)

Menu → Quality & Safety → HSE
  • Incidents — log near-misses, first aid, medical, lost-time, fatality or property damage (type, severity, location, investigation, root cause).
  • Safety Inspections — checklist inspections with a Pass/Fail result and score.
  • Toolbox Talks — record safety talks, presenter and attendance.
  • Risk Assessments — activity-based assessments with a risk level and validity dates.
  • PPE & PPE Checks — issue PPE and run pass/fail checks per worker.

The safety dashboard shows the incident frequency rate and a safety score.

18 · Risk Register

Menu → Quality & Safety → Risk Register

Log project risks with a probability (1–5) and impact (1–5); the score (probability × impact) is colour-coded (green < 8, amber 8–14, red ≥ 15). Record the mitigation and owner, and track status (Open / Mitigating / Closed).

19 · Approvals

Menu → Workflow & Records → Approvals

Requests that need sign-off (e.g. purchase requests) appear here. Approvers click Approve or Reject with a note; the requester is notified. You need the approval permission (Admins, Project Managers and Quantity Surveyors) to action them.

20 · Documents

Menu → Workflow & Records → Documents
  • Evidence Register — project-scoped evidence attached to records (GRNs, issues, inspections, IPCs, etc.).
  • Documents — a versioned library for drawings, certificates and contracts; add a document with its category and file link, and new versions keep the history.

21 · Reports & Exports

Menu → Workflow & Records → Reports · or the Excel button on any list
  • From the Reports page, download portfolio and per-project reports.
  • From any list, click Excel to export exactly what you’re viewing (your search, filters and dates are applied).

Cross-module reporting is available to System Administrators, Project Managers and Quantity Surveyors.

22 · AI Copilot

Menu → AI Copilot · or “Ask AI” in the top bar
  1. Open the Copilot and type a question in plain language, e.g. “Which project has the worst cost variance?” or “Summarise this month’s payroll.”
  2. The Copilot answers using your live data and is aware of the page you’re on.
💡 Try asking “Show under-performing activities”, “Any low-stock materials?”, “Open NCRs and their cost”.

The Copilot and Executive Intelligence require the AI permission (Admins, Project Managers, Quantity Surveyors).

23 · Dashboards & Portfolio

  • Dashboard — your home page: project health, KPIs and AI insights.
  • Executive Intelligence — AI-blended company-wide metrics for leadership.
  • Portfolio — compare all projects side by side (progress, cost variance, top risk).
  • Planning Dashboards — resource, labour and material planning roll-ups.

24 · Administration Admins only

Menu → Administration
  • Users — invite team members and set their role.
  • Roles — view exactly what each role can access.
  • Company Settings — name, currency (RWF), TIN, logo, working days.
  • Audit Trail — a record of who created, changed or deleted what, including sign-ins.

25 · Tips & FAQ

I signed up but can’t sign in

You must click the verification link in the email we sent first. Use Resend on the sign-in screen if you didn’t receive it, and check your spam folder. Links expire after 24 hours.

I don’t see a menu item

Your role may not have access to it. Ask your System Administrator to adjust your role in Administration → Users.

A list says “select a project”

Choose a project from the dropdown at the top of the screen.

How do I see the guided tour?

Click the “?” (Help) button in the top bar at any time.

Can I export my data?

Yes — every list has an Excel button, and the Reports page produces portfolio and project reports.

What currency is used?

Rwandan Francs (RWF) throughout.

Is my data safe?

Access is controlled by role, every change is recorded in the audit trail, and your company’s data is kept completely separate from other companies.
